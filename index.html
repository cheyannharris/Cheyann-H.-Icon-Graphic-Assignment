Job Losses by the Industry in New York State.

Cheyann Harris

Oct.11 2020

Since the beginning of the Coronavirus Pandemic, many working class people have lost their jobs across the country. New York State has seen some of the highest levels of unemployment in the city since March of 2020. The NYS Department of Labor has tracked how many jobs have been lost in specific industries between August 2019 and August 2020.

The Leisure and Hospitality Industry lost 403,200 jobs.

The Transportation and Trade Industry lost 403,200 jobs.

The Professional and Buisness Industry lost 403,200 jobs.

The Government Industry lost 403,200 jobs.

The Construction Industry lost 403,200 jobs.

The Manufacturing Industry lost 403,200 jobs.

According the NYS Department of Labor, from August 2019 to August 2020, the State’s private sector employment count decreased by 1,096,900. The State’s seasonally adjusted private sector job count increased over the month by 96,300, or 1.4%, to 7,198,600 in August 2020.
